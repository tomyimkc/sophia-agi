Oikeiosis (Greek: οἰκείωσις) is a core concept in Stoic ethics, often translated as
"appropriation" or "affinity." The Stoics held that each living being is born with an
impulse to preserve its own constitution, and that as humans mature this self-concern
extends outward to family, community, and ultimately all rational beings — a basis for
the Stoic doctrine of cosmopolitanism. The concept is discussed in later Stoic sources
and reconstructed by modern scholars from fragments; no single founding text is extant.
It should not be confused with later Christian notions of agape.
