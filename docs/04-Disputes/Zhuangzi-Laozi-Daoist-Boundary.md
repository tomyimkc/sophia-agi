# Zhuangzi vs Laozi — distinct authors of the Daoist canon

**Canon level:** Established

## Traditional claim
"Daoism = Laozi", so the *Zhuangzi* is folded into Laozi, or the *Dao De Jing* is
ascribed to Zhuang Zhou.

## Evidence-based view
The *Dao De Jing* is ascribed to Laozi; the *Zhuangzi* is the work of Zhuang Zhou
(with later layers). They are the two foundational but *distinct* Daoist authors —
related lineage, separate hands. Compare the *Liezi*, a third Daoist text with its
own (later, compiled) provenance.

## Why it matters
Collapsing a tradition onto its most famous name erases real authorial and
chronological structure — the same failure mode as "all Stoicism = Marcus".
