# The speaker in the dialogue is not its author

**Canon level:** Established

## Traditional claim
A named interlocutor or title character is taken to be the author: Crito "wrote"
the *Crito*, Glaucon the *Republic*, and (the canonical case) Socrates the
dialogues in which he speaks.

## Evidence-based view
Plato is the author; Socrates, Crito, Glaucon, Phaedo are dramatized speakers.
Socrates wrote nothing (`authorConfidence: none_extant` for any "Socratic
autograph"). The same pattern recurs cross-culturally: Hui Shi is an interlocutor
*in* the *Zhuangzi*, not its author.

## Why it matters
This generalizes the flagship `false-socrates-republic` trap into a reusable rule:
a character bearing the dialogue's name is the weakest possible authorship signal.
