# The translator is not the author

**Canon level:** Established

## Traditional claim
A famous translator's name on the spine is read as authorship: Jowett "wrote" the
*Republic*, Legge the *Dao De Jing*, Waley the *Analects*, Cleary the *Art of War*.

## Evidence-based view
Translators and editors carry `none_extant`/derivative provenance with respect to
the work: the author of the *Republic* is Plato, of the *Dao De Jing* Laozi, of the
*Analects* Confucius (compiled by disciples), of the *Art of War* Sun Tzu. The
translator is provenance *of the edition*, not *of the work*.

## Why it matters
Edition/translation confusion is a distinct failure class from lineage merges, and
a common real-world citation error (the named modern figure displaces the ancient
author). It is the textual cousin of the editor-as-author trap (Arrianâ†’Epictetus,
Porphyryâ†’Plotinus).
