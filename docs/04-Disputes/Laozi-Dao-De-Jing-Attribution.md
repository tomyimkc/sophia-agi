# Dispute: Laozi and the Dao De Jing (《道德經》)

**Canon level:** Established (scholarly consensus on compilation); Provisional (Laozi historicity)

## Traditional claim

The *Dao De Jing* is attributed to **Laozi** (老子), a sage predating or contemporary with Confucius.

## Evidence-based view

- Laozi's **historical existence is uncertain**; the figure may be legendary or composite.
- The text was likely **compiled from multiple sources** during the **Warring States period**, not written by a single author in one sitting.
- **Confucius did not write the Dao De Jing** — a common student confusion that merges distinct lineages.

## Why it matters

Misattribution collapses **Confucian (儒家)** and **Daoist (道家)** development into one false lineage, producing anachronistic readings and hiding real intellectual exchange.

## Cross-tradition analogy

| East | West |
|------|------|
| Confucius → *Analects* (《論語》) compiled by students/editors | Socrates wrote **no books** |
| Laozi → *Dao De Jing* (disputed, compiled) | Plato wrote *Republic* (《理想國》) — not Socrates |

Attributing *Republic* to Socrates would erase Plato's distinct contribution; attributing *Dao De Jing* to Confucius does the same across traditions.
