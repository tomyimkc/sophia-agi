# Dispute: Mencius (《孟子》) authorship

**Canon level:** Established (Mencius as figure); Provisional (exact compilation layers)

## Common trap

Students and LLMs attribute **《孟子》** to **Confucius** (孔子), collapsing two centuries of Confucian development into one voice.

## Evidence-based view

- **Confucius did not write the Mencius.** The text is attributed to **Mencius** (孟子, Meng Ke), a Warring States Confucian thinker.
- Like the *Analects*, the received text was **compiled and edited** over time — not a single autograph manuscript.
- Mencius extends Confucian moral philosophy; merging him with Confucius obscures doctrinal change (e.g. innate goodness debates).

## Cross-tradition analogy

| East | West |
|------|------|
| Confucius → *Analects* (compiled) | Socrates → no extant books |
| Mencius → *Mencius* (later Confucian) | Plato → *Symposium* (dialogue, not Socrates' authorship) |
