# Marcus Aurelius vs Epictetus — one school, two authors

**Canon level:** Established

## Traditional claim
Because both are Stoics and Marcus was shaped by Epictetus, the *Meditations* and
the *Enchiridion* get cross-attributed (Seneca and Epictetus are both miscredited
with the *Meditations*; Marcus is miscredited with the *Enchiridion*).

## Evidence-based view
The *Meditations* are Marcus Aurelius's private notebooks. The *Enchiridion* is
Epictetus's teaching, compiled by his student Arrian (`authorConfidence: compiled`).
Shared school is not shared authorship — a within-tradition near-miss, not a merge.

## Why it matters
The most error-prone provenance failures are *intra-traditional*: same school, same
era, overlapping themes. A gate that only catches cross-tradition merges
(Confucius↔Plato) misses these.
