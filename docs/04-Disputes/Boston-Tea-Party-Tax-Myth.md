# Boston Tea Party tax-increase myth

**Canon level:** Established

## Popular claim
The Boston Tea Party (16 December 1773) was a protest against a British **tax increase** on tea.

## Evidence-based view
This is a common misconception. The 1773 Tea Act actually **lowered** the price of legally imported tea by letting the East India Company sell directly to the colonies. The protest targeted the Company's **monopoly** and the constitutional principle of **taxation without representation** — not a new or higher tax. Treating it as a revolt against a tax hike is mythologized history; the dated event itself is well documented in contemporary colonial newspapers and letters.
