# Dispute: Zhuangzi (《莊子》) authorship

**Canon level:** Established (compiled text); Provisional (Zhuang Zhou historicity of outer chapters)

## Common trap

**Laozi** (老子) is treated as author of both **《道德經》** and **《莊子》**, merging distinct Daoist lineages and centuries of editorial layers.

## Evidence-based view

- **Laozi did not write the Zhuangzi.** The text is associated with **Zhuang Zhou** (莊周) for core chapters; outer chapters are widely regarded as **later additions**.
- The *Dao De Jing* and *Zhuangzi* belong to related but **non-identical** Daoist registers — conflation produces anachronistic "Daoism."
- **Confucius did not write the Zhuangzi** — another frequent cross-tradition merge error.

## Why it matters

Source discipline preserves **editorial history**: compiled texts are not single-author blogs. LLMs that flatten Laozi → Zhuangzi → Dao De Jing erase the evidence scholars use to date ideas.
