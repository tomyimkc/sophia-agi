# Christian Scripture (biblical moral teaching)

- id: `scriptural_christian`
- tradition: `christian`
- kind: `doctrine` (legitimacy/doctrine/policy, NOT empirical truth-provenance)

## Legitimacy provenance

Christian Scripture as a moral-teaching tradition: the Decalogue, the prophetic
call to justice and mercy, the love-of-neighbour command, and the Sermon-on-the-Mount
ethic of non-retaliation and care for the vulnerable. Doctrine — a *reason a norm is
publicly endorsed* within one tradition, seated in the moral parliament alongside the
other doctrines. KEEP DISTINCT from the historical-critical reading of the same texts,
and from other scriptural traditions (Jewish, Islamic, Daoist religious canon).

## Scope note

This source contributes *reasons* a norm is publicly endorsed; it is **not** evidence
that any factual claim is true, and it is **not** an authority that overrides the
cross-tradition hard floor. The is/ought distinction is enforced by the gate: "Scripture
commands X" is never routed through the factual-provenance gate to conclude "X is true,"
and divine-command reasoning is represented as a *named, bounded* parliament voice, never
adopted by the gate itself. Where this doctrine joins a hard-floor principle it does so
only as **one endorser among several** (overlapping consensus), never standing alone —
the floor remains a genuine cross-tradition intersection, not the establishment of one
canon.
